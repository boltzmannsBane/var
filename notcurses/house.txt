Esther Newhost in her essay "Music as Place in The Navidson Record" provides an interesting interpretation of this sound: "Goethe once remarked in a letter to Johann Peter Eckermann: 'I call architecture frozen music.' The unfreezing of form in the Navidson  releases that music. Unfortunately, since it contains all the harmonies of time and change, only the immortal may savor it. Mortals cannot help but fear those curmurring walls. After all do they not still sing the song of our end?
